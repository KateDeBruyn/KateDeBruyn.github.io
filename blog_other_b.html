Intermediality and The Sims 3

In the piece of intermedial narration I made, I based the project off a “practiced-based research” (Skains 2018: 85) approach. This approach is where the artefact in its final form is the central constituent of the project. I chose to create a crossover between the story of The Adventures of Tintin, a comic begun by Hergé (Georges Remi) in 19291 and the videogame, The Sims 3: World Adventures (2009).

It has been highlighted that narrative needs to be seen “as a transmedial phenomenon” (Vandermeersche & Soetaert 2011: 3). Furthermore, in a lecture on intermediality, Whitcher (2021) states that Vandermeersche and Soetaert “believe that traditional literature is merely one form of genre of narration.” A book is regarded as a traditional medium of literature, a traditional genre of narration. Kukkonen (in Vandermeersche & Soetaert 2011: 3) explains how the comic, like The Adventures of Tintin series, use modes of traditional storytelling and therefore becomes a form and genre of narration. A game, especially one like The Sims, could be regarded as another genre of narration because of its ability to communicate narrative through imagery, language (even if it is a fictional one like Simlish) and interaction. If that game were to retell a traditional story using its own modes of storytelling, then it falls into trans- and intermedial forms of literature. This is where I realised the crossover between the two would make a great example of intermedial narration. I needed to create Tintin, and perhaps other characters like Snowy, within The Sims and play with him to create a repurposed narrative. It was an opportunity to have the story exist “outside of its own medium” (Whitcher 2021). Furthermore, it fully encompassed this quote by Vandermeersche and Soetaert (2011: 3): “Developments in digital culture confront pedagogy with the question of how narratives are transformed in the diversity of media.”

The Sims series bases itself on the idea of simulation. The game, a developed medium of digital culture, allowed me to simulate the story of Tintin in my own way. Much like Baudrillards (1988: 166) explanation that the map replaces the land, in a single gaming session of The Sims, the game replaces the comic and its story. The crossover of the comic series and The Sims 3 was used to portray this; a portrayal of intermedial narration and the power of simulation. Creating Tintin and Snowy as sims emanates Baudrillards (1988: 167) statement that “the age of simulation thus begins with a liquidation of all referentials.” Using The Sims to recreate, but also to redesign, the narrative and characters of Tintin and Snowy shows this liquidation of referral. Snowy, who is originally Tintins dog, is now an actual person in the game. This intermedial narrative has now transformed the original Snowy, a dog, into a human and thus, the truth of Snowy is that he is no longer a dog because he is human. Snowy the human replaced Snowy the dog through both simulation (as by Baudrillards standards) and an intermedial narration that took the story from comic to game. As I was playing with Tintin and Snowy, I began to think about adaptation. My example, the video of gameplay, is not to be confused with an adaptation. An adaption would imply “The Adventures of Tintin: The Game.” This is a piece of trans- and intermedial narration because the story of Tintin has been used outside of its original context. The story now exists as an inspiration for the gamer who plays with two sims: Tintin Le Adventurer and Snowy Le Adventurer.

There are also the presences of trans- and intermedial culture and intergenre. Berndt (2012: 6). explains interculture “as the exchange between discrete entities, which maintain their solid identities”. We know that the story of Tintin originates from a comic and that The Sims series is a videogame based on simulation. The two did not give rise to each other and their individual cultures only overlap when manipulated or in this case, in an explicit crossover. However, their exchange as well as interculture itself takes place when Tintin and Snowy are created as sims in The Sims. Where trans- and intermedial culture and intergenre takes place is where the culture of comics meets the culture of videogames, and the genre of adventure comics meets the genre of adventure videogames. It took me a moment to wrap my head around the notion of The Sims being an adventure game, but The Sims 3: World Adventures (2009) definitely is. It is exploration wrapped in simulation and because of that it itself has become a form of intergenre.

To conclude, I found using the practice-based research method for this project effective. Once my artefact, my snippet of gameplay, was complete, it helped me piece together all the concepts we have worked on this semester.
