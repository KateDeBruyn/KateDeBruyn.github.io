First Wireframe

This initial wireframe design is what inspired the grid-like theme I have at the moment. On the draft, I began with two images next to one another and borders at the top and bottom. Looking at it again without "seeing" the contents, it immediately struck me as a grid. The grid theme feels well organised which I feel helps make the content; the text and images, easy to read.

Stylesheet

I knew I wanted a black background for my website to begin with. I then wanted a colour that was easy to read against a black, which is typically white, but started to have a look at the dark theme I use for applications on my computer. Visual Code and Visual Studio use a lot of blue, green, purple and a peachy-orange colour. I liked the blue a lot so I decided to try out a few hues and tones. I realise now that I also like the coding aesthetic - the typewriter font and all the symbols and variables. I may head towards a style closer to that because it also represents my interest in programming and developing. The site already some computer-esque style, blue against black being a common theme, so I may try double down on that once I'm more familiar with html, CSS, and JavaScript.
